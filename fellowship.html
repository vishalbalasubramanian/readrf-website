READ Research Fellowship

The READ Research Fellowship is a 6-month virtual program for students at undergraduate, Master's, and doctoral levels who are committed to research at the intersection of AI engineering, hardware, and public policy. Fellows collaborate with READ researchers and external mentors to produce publishable work, practical guidance, and policy-friendly briefs.

Program Overview

Fellows are matched to projects that span topics such as responsible AI design, explainability techniques for embedded systems, hardware-aware auditing, and governance frameworks for edge AI. The program emphasizes mentorship, collaborative research, and public dissemination.

Highlights & Benefits

  • Co-authorship on publications and whitepapers
  • Formal affiliation as a Research Fellow at READ
  • Mentorship from industry and academic experts
  • Certificates and letters of recommendation
  • Opportunities to present findings to policymakers and industry partners

Eligibility

Undergraduate (final year), Master's, and PhD students with demonstrated interest in AI, systems engineering, ethics, or policy. Prior research experience is helpful but not required.

Timeline

6 months (part-time). Typical timeline: onboarding & literature review (weeks 1–4), development & experiments (weeks 5–16), write-up & dissemination (weeks 17–24).

Deliverables

Working paper or technical report, reproducible artifacts (code/notebooks), policy brief or guidance document, and a presentation for READ convenings.

Mentorship & Evaluation

Each fellow is paired with a senior researcher and a policy mentor. Progress is evaluated through monthly deliverables, code reviews, and a final evaluation that informs authorship and continuation opportunities.

How to Apply

There is no application form hosted here — please contact us at contact@readfoundation.org with a CV, a short statement of research interests (300–500 words), and optionally a short writing sample or code link.

Stipend & Funding

Selected fellows receive a modest stipend to support research expenses. Funding varies by cohort and project; successful applicants will receive exact details with the offer letter. We also provide small travel grants for in-person dissemination when needed.

Intellectual Property & Publications

READ encourages open, reproducible research. Publication and IP decisions are made jointly between fellows and supervising researchers and will follow standard academic practices. READ supports preprints, open-source artifacts, and policy briefs; any exceptions are discussed during onboarding.

Evaluation & Outcomes

Fellows are evaluated on research progress, quality of deliverables, and collaboration skills. Typical successful outcomes include co-authored papers, technical reports, reproducible code artifacts, and policy-facing documents.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Is prior experience required?
No — motivation and a clear research interest statement are most important. We consider a range of backgrounds.
Can I participate remotely?
Yes. The program is primarily virtual, with optional in-person activities.
Will I receive authorship credit?
Authorship is determined by contribution and follows academic norms agreed with supervisors.
